The Livability Score

Introduction

The Livability Score is a score out of 100 designed to help people evaluate the quality of an area. It includes 9 categories and data from multiple datasets, ranking over 45,000 locations.

Each category (amenities, commute, cost of living, crime, employment, health & safety, housing, schools and user ratings) is scored individually based on data in the methodology below. A letter grade is assigned to each category in order to easily evaluate the score.

  • A+: 83–100 A: 80–82 A-: 77–79
  • B+: 74–76 B: 71–73 B: -68–70
  • C+: 65–67 C: 62–64 C: -59–61
  • D+: 56–58 D: 53–55
  • D-: 50–52 F: 0–49

Categories

Amenities

TThe amenities score provides an overview of the availability of local amenities in close proximity to an area. The algorithm searches for all amenities in the categories below within a 2 mile/3km (30 minute walk) radius to the center point of a location. Higher scores are awarded to the areas with the most nearby amenities from each of the following categories.

  • Coffee Shops: 14%
  • Entertainment: 14%

Sources

The livability score is sourced from AreaVibes, which use data from OpenStreetMap, US Census Bureau, and other authoritative sources to calculate the scores.
